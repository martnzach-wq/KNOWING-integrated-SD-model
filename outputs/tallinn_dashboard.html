Tallinn Integrated Model — Mobility · Energy Demand · Energy Supply, 2019–2050

Tallinn re-built on the v8 generic architecture (the same code that runs Granollers), using Tallinn's own original calibration data: mob_inputs.csv / mob_inp10.xlsx (mobility), EN_MEADCityInput.xlsx (energy demand), Supply_Data_Tallinn.xlsx (energy supply).

Scope note on the Granollers comparison: the actual Granollers v8 script could not be re-executed here (its config.json and EN_MEADCityInput_0527_v6.xlsx inputs were not available in this session). Granollers figures shown throughout are the script's own embedded reference checkpoints (verbatim from its cross-check assertions), not independently re-derived — and the two cities differ enormously in scale (Tallinn ~550k vs Granollers ~60–76k population) and climate (CDD≈15–40 vs ≈380). Side-by-side panels are for context, not as a validation target.

TallinnGranollers Granollers' car-fossil km/cap declines faster in relative terms after 2030 — its PI=1 trajectory (including EV adoption to 80% by 2050, per NECP/T&E projections) was explicitly tuned during that project's own calibration work, including capping several policy intensities at their 2040 values to avoid an unrealistic car-stock collapse. Tallinn's PI=1 initially ran into the same issue when every mob_inp10.xlsx policy lever was applied at full simultaneous strength (car share collapsed to ~1% by 2050) — but rather than capping individual policy levers, this was corrected with a general damping factor on the mode-change response itself (mode_change_damping = 0.245, calibrated so PI=1 car share lands near the ~20% reported from an earlier Vensim run of this model). This affects the speed of mode-shift uniformly across all mode pairs, without favouring any one policy lever.
PKM method comparison — centroid vs. mode-specific (Tallinn, PI=1)
km/cap/yr · the open methodological question flagged for this build
The original Tallinn Vensim data (mob_inputs.csv) only provides trip-length-class centroids (short/medium/long), shared across all modes — the same architecture Granollers started from before its v8 fix replaced it with empirical, mode-specific distances (TR_TripLength[zone,mode]) because the centroid approach materially distorted car/PT splits there. For Tallinn, a mode-specific variant was derived from TR InitialTravelSpeed[mode,trip length,traffic type] combined with car-anchored travel time per trip-length class. The two methods diverge most for PT (2,267 vs 3,426 km/cap/yr in 2019) — this divergence is presented for review, not resolved by this build.
EV fleet share — evidence-anchored trajectory
% of Tallinn's car fleet · historical anchors 2019–2026, policy-dependent ramp to 2050
EV fleet share (current scenario)
Corrected this session The original Vensim formula (TR EVChangeRate, gated by Time>120 months) produced exactly 0% through 2028 when run from a 2019 start — contradicted by Estonia's real, already-underway EV uptake. This was replaced with a trajectory anchored to actual historical data (elektriauto.ee / EAFO cumulative registrations: ~0.03% in 2019, ~0.2% in 2022, ~1.0% in 2024, ~1.1% in 2026 fleet share — distinct from the new-registration share, which is already far higher, ~9.7% in 2024) and, from 2026 onward, ramped toward Estonia's own stated policy ambition: a proposed ban on new petrol/diesel sales from 2030 and hybrid sales from 2035. PI=1 reaches 85% by 2050 (high end of an explicitly chosen 70–90%+ range); BAU reaches a damped ~43%, reflecting organic market growth without sustained policy reinforcement. Both figures carry real uncertainty — the EU diluted its own 2035 100%-zero-emission mandate to a 90% reduction requirement in December 2025, allowing hybrids/ICE with offsets beyond 2035, which makes a literal 100% outcome for Estonia's national ban look less certain even if formally adopted.
02 Energy demand submodel

Reads EN_MEADCityInput.xlsx (CETS sheet) for all non-transport sectors as-is. Transport final energy is recomputed from mobility-submodel distance output × evidence-based, Tallinn-specific conversion factors (car fossil ≈0.75 kWh/km from Estonia's above-EU-average fleet fuel consumption; car electric ≈0.222 kWh/km, a cold-climate annual average; public transport ≈0.040–0.050 kWh/pkm, blended from Tallinn's tram/trolleybus/bus fleet and Elron commuter rail) — the XLSX's own conversion-factor rows were found to be internally inconsistent with its own energy rows (a circular-dependency issue already flagged in project history for Granollers) and so were not used.

03 Energy supply & emissions

Translated from Submodel_EnergySupply_v4.mdl: full multi-technology dispatch (PV, Biomass CHP, Waste CHP, fossil peakers phasing out within 10 years, district-heat Heat Pump + Electric Boiler covering residual gap) — Tallinn, unlike Granollers, has a real district-heating network. The grid emission factor was deliberately overridden: the .mdl's own built-in simplification ("Average for Estonia: 0.4, going linear towards zero until 2050") was replaced with an evidence-based trajectory (0.78→0.02 kg CO₂/kWh, anchored to Ember/EEA reported values for Estonia's oil-shale-dominated grid), the same way Granollers replaced the .mdl's generic EU-average default with Spain's REE/PNIEC/PROENCAT path.

TallinnMatched to Granollers structureComponent 5 is Tallinn-specific Components 1, 2 and 4 use the identical factors as the Granollers v8 script (270 t CO₂/GWh direct combustion; grid import × each city's own grid emission factor). Component 3 (local fossil power) uses the .mdl's blended 0.55 kg/kWh factor rather than Granollers' gas-CHP-specific 0.45 kt/GWh, since Tallinn's local fossil capacity mixes oil/gas/peat/biogas plant rather than gas CHP alone. Component 5 (district heating fossil) has no Granollers equivalent — Granollers sets this to exactly zero because it has no DH network at all, while Tallinn has a real one with gas and oil boilers that are still operating (phasing out by ~2048 per the capacity trajectory) — this was missing from an earlier version of this build and added once identified, raising 2019 total emissions from 2,525.6 to 2,710.7 kt CO₂ and improving the match against Tallinn's own SECAP city-wide inventory (2,970 kt CO₂) from 85.0% to 91.3% coverage.

Note: Granollers' own component-level breakdown (em_transport, em_other, em_elec_prod, em_elec_imp individually) could not be re-derived here since the Granollers v8 script's config.json and XLSX inputs were not available this session — only its electricity-only subtotal (V4_EM) was embedded in the script as a hardcoded cross-check, shown separately above.
